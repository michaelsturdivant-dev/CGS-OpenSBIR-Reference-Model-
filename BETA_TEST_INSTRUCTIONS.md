# CGS Website and OpenSBIR Beta Test Instructions

## Purpose

These instructions guide beta testers through the Closed Gap Solutions public website, OpenSBIR reference model, and public-safe buyer journey.

The beta test validates whether a first-time visitor understands the CGS offer, trusts the public proof layer, and knows how to buy, book, request, or review support without confusion.

## Positioning Standard

Closed Gap Solutions helps organizations identify operational gaps, structure readiness plans, build implementation systems, and convert ideas into executable programs, proposals, dashboards, and decision-support artifacts.

CGS supports technical founders, small businesses, ministries, nonprofits, workforce programs, and community partners through structured intake, readiness assessments, implementation planning, knowledge management, and AI-supported administrative execution.

## What Beta Testers Should Evaluate

Evaluate CGS as an operational readiness, administrative intelligence, technical readiness, grant/proposal readiness, and implementation-support platform.

Do not evaluate the site as a legacy training company. The current public offer is readiness, implementation, and operational intelligence support.

Confirm whether the site clearly explains:

- What CGS does
- Who CGS serves
- What OpenSBIR Readiness Sprint is
- How to buy, book, request, or review proof
- Whether old legacy training language has been fully removed
- Whether the public/private architecture is clear

## Architecture Explanation for Testers

- Wix is the public engagement and front-door layer.
- GitHub is the public proof and reference model layer.
- Replit supports MVP and prototype interaction.
- Sentinel is the protected internal execution layer.

The public site and repository should display capabilities without exposing proprietary Sentinel logic, private client data, protected templates, internal prompts, clinical or billing logic, or credentialing workflows.

## Beta Test Path 1: First Impression

Start on the CGS homepage.

In the first 30 seconds, answer:

- Do you understand what CGS offers?
- Do you understand what OpenSBIR means?
- Do you understand that this is readiness and implementation support, not a guaranteed award service?
- Is the intended buyer clear?
- Which button would you click first?

What I thought OpenSBIR meant:

## Beta Test Path 3: Buyer Journey

Tester role:

> I am a small business, technical founder, researcher, ministry, nonprofit, or community partner interested in readiness support.

Complete this journey:

1. Land on the homepage.
2. Understand the CGS offer.
3. Understand the OpenSBIR Readiness Sprint.
4. Click the most logical next step.
5. Submit a request, reach a booking path, reach a payment path, or review the GitHub proof layer.
6. Confirm whether the next step is clear.

Record:

```text
Could I complete the journey? Yes / No
Where did I get stuck?
Did the site explain the difference between assessment, sprint, implementation support, GitHub proof, and Sentinel protected execution?
What would make me trust CGS more?
What would make me buy or book now?
```

## Beta Test Path 7: Trust and Guardrails

Confirm whether the site clearly communicates:

- CGS is a private company.
- CGS does not guarantee awards.
- CGS is not a government agency.
- GitHub is public proof, not the private execution engine.
- Sentinel is protected and not publicly exposed.
- Client-specific information is not placed in the public repository.

Record:

```text
Private company status clear? Yes / No
No-award-guarantee language clear? Yes / No
Public proof vs protected execution clear? Yes / No
I would trust CGS with my idea: Yes / No / Maybe
Why?
```

- 3 = Usable but confusing
- 2 = Major issue

## Beta Exit Criteria

The site is beta-ready when:

1. A first-time visitor understands CGS in under 30 seconds.
2. The visitor knows whether to buy, book, request, or review proof.
3. All CTAs work.
4. Forms route correctly.
5. Booking or payment paths work if activated.
6. Disclaimer and no-award-guarantee language are clear.
7. Old legacy training language has been removed.
8. Older pages do not confuse the current CGS offer.
9. Mobile navigation works.
10. Public assets do not expose proprietary Sentinel logic.
